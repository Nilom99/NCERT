Chapter 7: Weather, Climate and Adaptations of Animals to Climate

Solutions to NCERT Questions

Question 1:

What is weather?

Answer:

Weather refers to the short-term atmospheric conditions in a specific area, including temperature, humidity, precipitation, and wind.

Question 2:

What is climate?

Answer:

Climate is the long-term average of weather conditions in a particular region over an extended period, typically 30 years or more.

Question 3:

What are the factors affecting climate?

Answer:

Factors affecting climate include latitude, altitude, distance from the sea, ocean currents, and prevailing winds.

Question 4:

How do animals adapt to climate?

Answer:

Animals adapt to climate through physical adaptations (e.g., fur, body size), behavioral adaptations (e.g., migration), and physiological changes (e.g., hibernation).

Question 5:

What is the significance of climate in the environment?

Answer:

Climate influences ecosystems, biodiversity, agricultural productivity, and water resources, impacting overall environmental health.

Question 6:

What is the water cycle?

Answer:

The water cycle is the continuous movement of water between the Earth's surface, atmosphere, and underground, involving processes such as evaporation, condensation, and precipitation.

Question 7:

How does the water cycle affect weather and climate?

Answer:

The water cycle affects weather and climate by regulating temperature, precipitation patterns, and humidity levels, influencing local and global climates.

Question 8:

What is global warming?

Answer:

Global warming is the long-term rise in Earth's average surface temperature due to increased greenhouse gas emissions from human activities.

Question 9:

What are the effects of climate change?

Answer:

Effects of climate change include rising sea levels, extreme weather events, habitat loss, and shifts in ecosystems and biodiversity.

Question 10:

What measures can be taken to mitigate climate change?

Answer:

Measures to mitigate climate change include reducing greenhouse gas emissions, promoting renewable energy, and increasing energy efficiency.

Question 11:

What are the adaptations of desert animals?

Answer:

Desert animals have adaptations such as water conservation mechanisms, nocturnal behavior, and body structures to reflect sunlight.

Question 12:

What are the adaptations of polar animals?

Answer:

Polar animals have adaptations like thick fur or blubber for insulation, white coloration for camouflage, and behaviors like migration.

Question 13:

What is the importance of biodiversity in ecosystems?

Answer:

Biodiversity is crucial for ecosystem stability, resilience, and functioning, providing services such as pollination, nutrient cycling, and habitat provision.

Question 14:

How does deforestation affect climate?

Answer:

Deforestation affects climate by increasing carbon dioxide levels, disrupting local weather patterns, and reducing biodiversity.

Question 15:

What are the impacts of urbanization on local climate?

Answer:

Urbanization impacts local climate by creating urban heat islands, altering rainfall patterns, and increasing pollution levels.

Question 16:

What is the greenhouse effect?

Answer:

The greenhouse effect is the process by which certain gases trap heat in the Earth's atmosphere, warming the planet.

Question 17:

What are the roles of oceans in regulating climate?

Answer:

Oceans regulate climate by absorbing carbon dioxide, distributing heat, and influencing weather patterns through currents.

Question 18:

What is sustainable development?

Answer:

Sustainable development is development that meets the needs of the present without compromising the ability of future generations to meet their own needs.

Question 19:

How can individuals contribute to climate change mitigation?

Answer:

Individuals can contribute to climate change mitigation by reducing energy consumption, using public transport, recycling, and supporting sustainable practices.

Question 20:

What is the significance of understanding climate and weather for society?

Answer:

Understanding climate and weather is vital for disaster preparedness, agricultural planning, and informed decision-making regarding environmental policies.
