Chapter 8: Winds, Storms and Cyclones

Solutions to NCERT Questions

Question 1:

What are winds?

Answer:

Winds are moving air that is created by differences in atmospheric pressure.

Question 2:

What is a cyclone?

Answer:

A cyclone is a large-scale air mass that rotates around a center of low atmospheric pressure, causing severe weather conditions.

Question 3:

What causes winds to blow?

Answer:

Winds blow due to the uneven heating of the Earth's surface by the sun, creating areas of high and low pressure.

Question 4:

What is the difference between a storm and a cyclone?

Answer:

A storm is a general term for severe weather conditions, while a cyclone specifically refers to a rotating system of winds around a low-pressure area.

Question 5:

What are the types of cyclones?

Answer:

The types of cyclones include tropical cyclones, extratropical cyclones, and polar cyclones, differing by their formation and location.

Question 6:

What are the impacts of cyclones?

Answer:

Impacts of cyclones include strong winds, heavy rainfall, storm surges, and damage to infrastructure and ecosystems.

Question 7:

How do cyclones form?

Answer:

Cyclones form over warm ocean waters where warm, moist air rises, creating low pressure and causing surrounding air to flow in.

Question 8:

What are the warning signs of a cyclone?

Answer:

Warning signs of a cyclone include darkening skies, strong winds, rising sea levels, and changes in air pressure.

Question 9:

What precautions should be taken during a cyclone?

Answer:

Precautions during a cyclone include staying indoors, securing loose objects, and following emergency instructions from authorities.

Question 10:

What is a storm surge?

Answer:

A storm surge is an abnormal rise in sea level due to strong winds and low pressure associated with cyclones, leading to coastal flooding.

Question 11:

How do storms affect agriculture?

Answer:

Storms can damage crops, erode soil, and disrupt planting and harvesting schedules, impacting agricultural productivity.

Question 12:

What is the role of the Indian Meteorological Department?

Answer:

The Indian Meteorological Department monitors weather patterns, provides forecasts, and issues warnings for extreme weather events like cyclones.

Question 13:

How can technology help in predicting cyclones?

Answer:

Technology helps in predicting cyclones through satellite imagery, radar systems, and computer models that analyze weather data.

Question 14:

What are the effects of climate change on cyclone patterns?

Answer:

Climate change can lead to more intense and frequent cyclones, as warmer ocean temperatures provide more energy for storm formation.

Question 15:

What should be included in a disaster preparedness plan for cyclones?

Answer:

A disaster preparedness plan should include emergency contacts, evacuation routes, supplies, and communication strategies.

Question 16:

What is the significance of understanding wind patterns?

Answer:

Understanding wind patterns is crucial for predicting weather, planning agriculture, and ensuring safe aviation and maritime operations.

Question 17:

How can communities prepare for storms and cyclones?

Answer:

Communities can prepare by conducting drills, establishing early warning systems, and educating residents on safety measures.

Question 18:

What are some examples of famous cyclones?

Answer:

Examples of famous cyclones include Hurricane Katrina, Typhoon Haiyan, and Cyclone Nargis, known for their devastating impacts.

Question 19:

What role do trees play in storm protection?

Answer:

Trees provide windbreaks, reduce soil erosion, and absorb rainwater, helping to mitigate storm impacts on land and communities.

Question 20:

What is the difference between a tornado and a cyclone?

Answer:

A tornado is a rapidly rotating column of air that extends from a thunderstorm to the ground, while a cyclone is a larger weather system characterized by rotating winds around a low-pressure center.
