Chapter 1: Nutrition in Plants

Solutions to NCERT Questions

Question 1:

What is photosynthesis?

Answer:

Photosynthesis is the process by which green plants use sunlight to synthesize foods with the help of chlorophyll, primarily producing glucose and oxygen from carbon dioxide and water.

Question 2:

What are the raw materials required for photosynthesis?

Answer:

The raw materials required for photosynthesis are carbon dioxide, water, and sunlight.

Question 3:

Why are leaves green in color?

Answer:

Leaves are green due to the presence of chlorophyll, a pigment that absorbs light energy needed for photosynthesis.

Question 4:

What is the significance of stomata in plants?

Answer:

Stomata are small openings on the surface of leaves that facilitate gas exchange, allowing carbon dioxide to enter and oxygen to exit during photosynthesis.

Question 5:

Explain the role of sunlight in photosynthesis.

Answer:

Sunlight provides the energy required for photosynthesis, enabling plants to convert carbon dioxide and water into glucose and oxygen.

Question 6:

What are the two types of nutrition in plants?

Answer:

The two types of nutrition in plants are autotrophic (where plants make their own food) and heterotrophic (where plants depend on other organisms for food).

Question 7:

What is the difference between autotrophic and heterotrophic nutrition?

Answer:

Autotrophic nutrition involves organisms producing their own food (e.g., plants), while heterotrophic nutrition involves organisms obtaining food from other sources (e.g., animals).

Question 8:

What is chlorophyll?

Answer:

Chlorophyll is a green pigment found in plants that plays a crucial role in absorbing light energy for photosynthesis.

Question 9:

Describe the process of photosynthesis.

Answer:

Photosynthesis occurs in two stages: the light-dependent reactions capture sunlight and produce ATP and NADPH, while the light-independent reactions (Calvin Cycle) use these products to convert carbon dioxide into glucose.

Question 10:

What are the products of photosynthesis?

Answer:

The primary products of photosynthesis are glucose and oxygen.

Question 11:

How do plants obtain water?

Answer:

Plants absorb water from the soil through their roots, which is then transported to the leaves for photosynthesis.

Question 12:

What are the main sources of carbon dioxide for plants?

Answer:

The main source of carbon dioxide for plants is the atmosphere, where it is obtained through the stomata during the process of photosynthesis.

Question 13:

What is the role of water in photosynthesis?

Answer:

Water is essential for photosynthesis as it provides electrons and protons needed in the light reactions and is also a byproduct of the process.

Question 14:

Explain the importance of photosynthesis for life on Earth.

Answer:

Photosynthesis is vital for life on Earth as it produces oxygen necessary for respiration and forms the basis of the food chain by providing energy to autotrophs and subsequently to heterotrophs.

Question 15:

What is the role of oxygen produced during photosynthesis?

Answer:

The oxygen produced during photosynthesis is released into the atmosphere and is essential for the survival of aerobic organisms that depend on it for respiration.

Question 16:

What are the factors affecting the rate of photosynthesis?

Answer:

The factors affecting the rate of photosynthesis include light intensity, carbon dioxide concentration, temperature, and water availability.

Question 17:

What is meant by the term "food chain"?

Answer:

A food chain is a linear sequence of organisms where each organism is eaten by the next in the chain, illustrating the flow of energy and nutrients in an ecosystem.

Question 18:

What are saprophytes?

Answer:

Saprophytes are organisms that obtain nutrients by feeding on dead and decaying organic matter, playing a key role in nutrient recycling in ecosystems.

Question 19:

What is the role of nitrogen-fixing bacteria in plants?

Answer:

Nitrogen-fixing bacteria convert atmospheric nitrogen into a form that plants can absorb and utilize, thereby enriching the soil and aiding plant growth.

Question 20:

What is the role of fungi in nutrition?

Answer:

Fungi act as decomposers in ecosystems, breaking down dead organic matter and returning nutrients to the soil, which is essential for plant nutrition.
