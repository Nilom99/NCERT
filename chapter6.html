Chapter 6: Physical and Chemical Changes

Solutions to NCERT Questions

Question 1:

What is a physical change?

Answer:

A physical change is a change in which the form or appearance of a substance changes, but its chemical composition remains the same.

Question 2:

What is a chemical change?

Answer:

A chemical change is a change that results in the formation of new substances with different chemical properties.

Question 3:

Give an example of a physical change.

Answer:

An example of a physical change is melting ice into water.

Question 4:

Give an example of a chemical change.

Answer:

An example of a chemical change is burning wood, which produces ash and smoke.

Question 5:

What are the characteristics of physical changes?

Answer:

Characteristics of physical changes include reversible processes, no new substances formed, and changes in physical properties such as shape and size.

Question 6:

What are the characteristics of chemical changes?

Answer:

Characteristics of chemical changes include irreversible processes, new substances formed, and changes in chemical properties.

Question 7:

How can we identify a chemical change?

Answer:

A chemical change can be identified by the release of gas, formation of a precipitate, change in color, or change in temperature.

Question 8:

What is the law of conservation of mass?

Answer:

The law of conservation of mass states that mass cannot be created or destroyed in a chemical reaction; it is conserved.

Question 9:

What happens to substances during a chemical change?

Answer:

During a chemical change, the bonds between atoms are broken and new bonds are formed, resulting in new substances.

Question 10:

What is rusting?

Answer:

Rusting is a chemical change that occurs when iron reacts with oxygen and moisture, forming iron oxide (rust).

Question 11:

What is the difference between a reactant and a product?

Answer:

Reactants are the starting substances in a chemical reaction, while products are the substances formed as a result of the reaction.

Question 12:

What is fermentation?

Answer:

Fermentation is a metabolic process that converts sugar to acids, gases, or alcohol in the absence of oxygen.

Question 13:

What is the significance of chemical changes in our daily life?

Answer:

Chemical changes are significant in cooking, digestion, and the production of various materials and substances we use daily.

Question 14:

What role do catalysts play in chemical reactions?

Answer:

Catalysts speed up chemical reactions without being consumed in the process, enabling reactions to occur more efficiently.

Question 15:

How can we prevent rusting?

Answer:

Rusting can be prevented by applying protective coatings, using rust-resistant alloys, and storing metals in dry environments.

Question 16:

What is photosynthesis?

Answer:

Photosynthesis is a chemical process by which green plants convert light energy into chemical energy in the form of glucose, using carbon dioxide and water.

Question 17:

What are endothermic and exothermic reactions?

Answer:

Endothermic reactions absorb heat from the surroundings, while exothermic reactions release heat.

Question 18:

What is the significance of chemical reactions in the environment?

Answer:

Chemical reactions play a crucial role in environmental processes, such as decomposition, nutrient cycling, and energy flow.

Question 19:

What is the role of enzymes in chemical reactions?

Answer:

Enzymes act as biological catalysts that speed up chemical reactions in living organisms without being consumed.

Question 20:

How do we use chemical changes in industry?

Answer:

Chemical changes are utilized in various industries for manufacturing, food processing, pharmaceuticals, and energy production.
