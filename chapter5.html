Chapter 5: Acids, Bases and Salts

Solutions to NCERT Questions

Question 1:

What are acids?

Answer:

Acids are substances that release hydrogen ions (H+) in solution and have a sour taste.

Question 2:

What are bases?

Answer:

Bases are substances that accept hydrogen ions or release hydroxide ions (OH-) in solution and have a bitter taste.

Question 3:

What are salts?

Answer:

Salts are compounds formed when acids react with bases, resulting in the neutralization of their properties.

Question 4:

What is the pH scale?

Answer:

The pH scale is a numerical scale ranging from 0 to 14 used to measure the acidity or alkalinity of a solution.

Question 5:

What is the significance of pH in daily life?

Answer:

pH is important in various aspects of daily life, such as maintaining soil quality for agriculture, human health, and chemical reactions.

Question 6:

What are indicators?

Answer:

Indicators are substances that change color when exposed to acids or bases, used to determine the pH of a solution.

Question 7:

Give an example of a natural indicator.

Answer:

Litmus is a natural indicator derived from lichens that changes color in the presence of acids (red) or bases (blue).

Question 8:

What is the reaction between an acid and a base called?

Answer:

The reaction between an acid and a base is called neutralization, resulting in the formation of water and a salt.

Question 9:

What happens when an acid reacts with a metal?

Answer:

When an acid reacts with a metal, it produces hydrogen gas and a salt.

Question 10:

What are the uses of acids in daily life?

Answer:

Acids have various uses, including in food preservation, cleaning agents, and battery production.

Question 11:

What is the role of bases in soap?

Answer:

Bases play a crucial role in soap production through a process called saponification, which involves the reaction of fats with an alkali.

Question 12:

Explain the importance of salts in our diet.

Answer:

Salts are essential for various bodily functions, including maintaining fluid balance, nerve transmission, and muscle contraction.

Question 13:

What is baking soda?

Answer:

Baking soda, or sodium bicarbonate, is a mild alkaline compound used in baking, cleaning, and as an antacid.

Question 14:

What is vinegar?

Answer:

Vinegar is a solution of acetic acid in water, commonly used in cooking, pickling, and as a condiment.

Question 15:

How can we test the acidity or alkalinity of a solution?

Answer:

We can test the acidity or alkalinity of a solution using pH strips or indicators like litmus paper.

Question 16:

What is the effect of acid rain on the environment?

Answer:

Acid rain can harm aquatic life, damage vegetation, and erode buildings and monuments.

Question 17:

What is the importance of maintaining pH in swimming pools?

Answer:

Maintaining proper pH levels in swimming pools is essential for comfort, preventing skin irritation, and ensuring the effectiveness of disinfectants.

Question 18:

What is the effect of temperature on solubility?

Answer:

Generally, the solubility of solids in liquids increases with temperature, while the solubility of gases decreases with an increase in temperature.

Question 19:

What is the significance of acids in digestion?

Answer:

Acids, such as hydrochloric acid in the stomach, aid in the digestion of food and kill harmful bacteria.

Question 20:

Explain the importance of neutralization reactions in agriculture.

Answer:

Neutralization reactions help to balance soil pH, improving nutrient availability and supporting healthy plant growth.
