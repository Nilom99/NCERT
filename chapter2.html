Chapter 2: Nutrition in Animals

Solutions to NCERT Questions

Question 1:

List the different parts of the human digestive system.

Answer:

The human digestive system consists of the mouth, esophagus, stomach, small intestine, large intestine, and anus. Each part plays a specific role in the digestion and absorption of nutrients.

Question 2:

Explain the role of teeth in the process of digestion.

Answer:

Teeth play a crucial role in the process of digestion by mechanically breaking down food into smaller pieces through chewing, which increases the surface area for enzymes to act upon during chemical digestion.

Question 3:

What are the different types of teeth in humans?

Answer:

Humans have four types of teeth: incisors (for cutting), canines (for tearing), premolars (for crushing), and molars (for grinding).

Question 4:

What is the function of saliva?

Answer:

Saliva helps in the digestion of carbohydrates through the enzyme amylase, moistens food for easier swallowing, and provides a medium for taste.

Question 5:

What is the significance of the stomach in digestion?

Answer:

The stomach secretes gastric juices containing hydrochloric acid and enzymes, which further break down food into a semi-liquid form called chyme.

Question 6:

Describe the process of absorption of nutrients.

Answer:

Nutrients are absorbed in the small intestine through villi, tiny finger-like projections that increase the surface area for absorption. Nutrients then enter the bloodstream for distribution.

Question 7:

What are herbivores? Give examples.

Answer:

Herbivores are animals that primarily consume plants. Examples include cows, goats, and rabbits.

Question 8:

Explain the role of the large intestine.

Answer:

The large intestine absorbs water and electrolytes from indigestible food matter and compacts waste into feces for elimination from the body.

Question 9:

What is the role of the pancreas in digestion?

Answer:

The pancreas produces digestive enzymes and bicarbonate that help neutralize stomach acid in the small intestine, aiding digestion.

Question 10:

Define saprophytic nutrition.

Answer:

Saprophytic nutrition is the mode of nutrition in which organisms obtain nutrients by feeding on dead and decaying organic matter. Fungi are typical examples of saprophytes.

Question 11:

What are carnivores? Give examples.

Answer:

Carnivores are animals that primarily consume other animals. Examples include lions, tigers, and eagles.

Question 12:

What is a balanced diet?

Answer:

A balanced diet contains all the essential nutrients—carbohydrates, proteins, fats, vitamins, and minerals—in the right proportions to maintain health and support growth.

Question 13:

How do animals obtain nutrients?

Answer:

Animals obtain nutrients by consuming food, which is then digested and absorbed into the body. Different animals have different feeding mechanisms.

Question 14:

What is the importance of dietary fibers?

Answer:

Dietary fibers aid in digestion, help maintain bowel health, lower cholesterol levels, and control blood sugar levels.

Question 15:

Explain the role of bile in digestion.

Answer:

Bile, produced by the liver and stored in the gallbladder, emulsifies fats into smaller droplets, making it easier for enzymes to digest them.

Question 16:

What is the function of gastric juice?

Answer:

Gastric juice contains hydrochloric acid and enzymes that help break down food, kill bacteria, and provide an acidic environment for digestive enzymes to work effectively.

Question 17:

What is absorption in the context of nutrition?

Answer:

Absorption is the process by which digested nutrients pass through the walls of the intestines into the bloodstream for use by the body.

Question 18:

How do omnivores obtain nutrition?

Answer:

Omnivores obtain nutrition by consuming both plant and animal-based foods, allowing them to have a diverse diet.

Question 19:

What are the symptoms of malnutrition?

Answer:

Symptoms of malnutrition may include fatigue, weakness, stunted growth, and increased susceptibility to infections.

Question 20:

What is the role of enzymes in digestion?

Answer:

Enzymes are biological catalysts that speed up the digestion of food by breaking down complex molecules into simpler ones, facilitating absorption.
