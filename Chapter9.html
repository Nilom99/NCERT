Chapter 9: Soil

Solutions to NCERT Questions

Question 1:

What is soil?

Answer:

Soil is a natural resource composed of organic matter, minerals, gases, liquids, and microorganisms that support plant life.

Question 2:

What are the different types of soil?

Answer:

The different types of soil include sandy soil, clayey soil, loamy soil, and silt soil, each with distinct properties and uses.

Question 3:

What is the composition of soil?

Answer:

Soil is composed of mineral particles, organic matter, water, and air, with the proportions varying by type.

Question 4:

How is soil formed?

Answer:

Soil is formed through the weathering of rocks, decomposition of organic matter, and the accumulation of minerals over time.

Question 5:

What are the characteristics of good soil?

Answer:

Good soil has a balanced composition of sand, silt, and clay, good drainage, sufficient organic matter, and a healthy population of microorganisms.

Question 6:

What is soil erosion?

Answer:

Soil erosion is the removal of the top layer of soil by wind, water, or human activity, which can lead to decreased fertility.

Question 7:

What are the causes of soil erosion?

Answer:

Causes of soil erosion include deforestation, overgrazing, urbanization, and poor agricultural practices.

Question 8:

What are the effects of soil erosion?

Answer:

Effects of soil erosion include loss of soil fertility, reduced agricultural productivity, sedimentation in waterways, and increased flooding.

Question 9:

How can we prevent soil erosion?

Answer:

Soil erosion can be prevented by planting cover crops, practicing crop rotation, building terraces, and maintaining vegetation cover.

Question 10:

What is the role of soil in the ecosystem?

Answer:

Soil plays a critical role in the ecosystem by providing nutrients to plants, storing water, supporting microbial life, and influencing the carbon cycle.

Question 11:

What are soil horizons?

Answer:

Soil horizons are layers of soil that differ in color, texture, structure, and composition, typically classified into O, A, E, B, and C horizons.

Question 12:

What is the importance of soil conservation?

Answer:

Soil conservation is important for maintaining soil health, preventing erosion, and ensuring sustainable agricultural practices.

Question 13:

What is organic farming?

Answer:

Organic farming is a method of agriculture that relies on natural processes and materials, avoiding synthetic fertilizers and pesticides.

Question 14:

How does soil quality affect plant growth?

Answer:

Soil quality affects plant growth by influencing nutrient availability, water retention, and root development.

Question 15:

What is soil fertility?

Answer:

Soil fertility refers to the ability of soil to provide essential nutrients to plants, influencing crop yield and health.

Question 16:

What are macronutrients and micronutrients in soil?

Answer:

Macronutrients are nutrients required in large quantities (e.g., nitrogen, phosphorus, potassium), while micronutrients are needed in smaller amounts (e.g., iron, zinc, copper).

Question 17:

What is composting?

Answer:

Composting is the process of decomposing organic waste to create nutrient-rich soil amendments.

Question 18:

How do earthworms benefit the soil?

Answer:

Earthworms benefit the soil by aerating it, improving drainage, and enhancing nutrient availability through their waste.
