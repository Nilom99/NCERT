Chapter 3: Fibre to Fabric

Solutions to NCERT Questions

Question 1:

What are fibers?

Answer:

Fibers are the basic building blocks of textiles. They can be natural or synthetic and are used to create fabrics for clothing and other products.

Question 2:

Differentiate between natural and synthetic fibers.

Answer:

Natural fibers are derived from plants and animals, while synthetic fibers are man-made and derived from chemical processes.

Question 3:

What is spinning?

Answer:

Spinning is the process of twisting fibers together to make yarn, which is used for weaving fabrics.

Question 4:

What are the two main types of natural fibers?

Answer:

The two main types of natural fibers are plant fibers (like cotton and jute) and animal fibers (like wool and silk).

Question 5:

What is weaving?

Answer:

Weaving is the process of interlacing two sets of yarn or threads at right angles to create a fabric.

Question 6:

Describe the process of dyeing.

Answer:

Dyeing is the process of adding color to textiles using various dyeing methods to enhance their aesthetic appeal.

Question 7:

What is the role of dyeing in textile production?

Answer:

Dyeing is the process of adding color to textiles. It enhances the aesthetic appeal of fabrics and is essential in the fashion industry.

Question 8:

What are the properties of natural fibers?

Answer:

Natural fibers are biodegradable, breathable, comfortable to wear, and often have good moisture absorption properties.

Question 9:

How is jute obtained? What are its uses?

Answer:

Jute is obtained from the stem of the jute plant. It is used to make bags, ropes, and carpets due to its strength and durability.

Question 10:

What is the significance of spinning in textile production?

Answer:

Spinning is the process of twisting fibers together to create yarn, which is essential for weaving fabric. It determines the strength and texture of the final product.

Question 11:

What are the differences between natural and synthetic fibers?

Answer:

Natural fibers are derived from plants and animals, while synthetic fibers are made from chemicals. Natural fibers are usually biodegradable, while synthetic fibers can take a long time to decompose.

Question 12:

Explain the role of finishing in textile production.

Answer:

Finishing involves processes that improve the appearance and performance of fabric, such as softening, waterproofing, and adding fire resistance.

Question 13:

How is silk produced?

Answer:

Silk is produced by silkworms that spin cocoons. The cocoons are harvested and boiled to extract the silk fibers, which are then spun into threads.

Question 14:

What is the environmental impact of synthetic fibers?

Answer:

Synthetic fibers can contribute to environmental pollution as they are derived from petroleum products and can take hundreds of years to decompose, leading to microplastic pollution.

Question 15:

What are the advantages of using natural fibers?

Answer:

Natural fibers are breathable, comfortable, biodegradable, and less likely to cause allergic reactions compared to synthetic fibers.

Question 16:

What is the purpose of carding in textile production?

Answer:

Carding is the process of untangling and cleaning fibers to prepare them for spinning, ensuring that the fibers are aligned and evenly distributed.

Question 17:

How do fibers contribute to the economy?

Answer:

Fibers contribute to the economy by supporting the textile industry, creating jobs, and providing raw materials for various products, including clothing and home textiles.

Question 18:

What are the main uses of cotton?

Answer:

Cotton is primarily used to make clothing, bed linens, towels, and other textile products due to its softness and breathability.

Question 19:

What factors influence the quality of fibers?

Answer:

The quality of fibers is influenced by factors such as the source of the fiber, the harvesting method, environmental conditions, and processing techniques.

Question 20:

How do the properties of fibers affect their applications?

Answer:

The properties of fibers, such as strength, durability, moisture absorption, and elasticity, determine their suitability for specific applications, such as clothing, upholstery, and industrial use.
